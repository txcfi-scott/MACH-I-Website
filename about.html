Our Mission

Aviation Medicine, Done Right

MACH I exists because pilots deserve cardiologists and pulmonologists who understand aviation. Not physicians who have to look up what a special issuance is. Not generalists who've never sat in a cockpit.

Dr. Davenport and Dr. Young are board-certified subspecialists who also happen to be military flight surgeons, FAA medical examiners, and active pilots. They don't just treat your heart or lungs — they understand what it means to lose your medical, and they know exactly what the FAA needs to give it back.

ED

Eddie D. Davenport, M.D., FACC, FAsMA

Founder & Medical Director

Dr. Davenport is a board-certified cardiologist who serves as the FAA's Cardiovascular Specialty Consultant — the physician the FAA calls when they need to know whether a pilot with a cardiac condition can safely fly. A retired USAF Colonel, he authored every cardiac aircrew waiver guide the Air Force has used since 2009.

He led the NATO Aviation Cardiology Working Group that produced the landmark 10-paper Heart journal series — the most comprehensive international aviation cardiology publication ever assembled. A licensed pilot with over 100 peer-reviewed publications, Dr. Davenport brings a combination of clinical expertise, military aviation medicine authority, and firsthand flying experience that is unmatched in aviation cardiology.

Adam Young, D.O.

Lead Pulmonologist

Dr. Young is triple board-certified in pulmonary medicine, critical care, and internal medicine. A USAF flight surgeon and aerospace medicine specialist, he brings the same rare combination as Dr. Davenport: subspecialty clinical expertise, military aviation medicine experience, FAA consulting authority, and firsthand understanding of what pilots face.

His experience in critical care air transport and aerospace physiology gives him unique insight into the pulmonary demands of flight — knowledge that directly informs his approach to FAA-ready evaluations for pilots with respiratory conditions.
