About Dr. Davenport

The FAA's Cardiovascular Specialist — Board-Certified Cardiologist, Military Flight Surgeon, and Licensed Pilot

Our Mission

Aviation Medicine, Done Right

MACH I exists because pilots deserve a cardiologist who understands aviation. Not a physician who has to look up what a special issuance is. Not a generalist who's never sat in a cockpit.

Dr. Davenport is a board-certified subspecialist who also happens to be a military flight surgeon, FAA medical examiner, and active pilot. He doesn't just treat your heart — he understands what it means to lose your medical, and he knows exactly what the FAA needs to give it back.

ED

Eddie D. Davenport, M.D., FACC, FAsMA

Founder & Medical Director

Dr. Davenport is a board-certified cardiologist who serves as the FAA's Cardiovascular Specialty Consultant — the physician the FAA calls when they need to know whether a pilot with a cardiac condition can safely fly. A retired USAF Colonel, he authored every cardiac aircrew waiver guide the Air Force has used since 2009.

He led the NATO Aviation Cardiology Working Group that produced the landmark 10-paper Heart journal series — the most comprehensive international aviation cardiology publication ever assembled. A licensed pilot with over 100 peer-reviewed publications, Dr. Davenport brings a combination of clinical expertise, military aviation medicine authority, and firsthand flying experience that is unmatched in aviation cardiology.
